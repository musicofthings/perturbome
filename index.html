“Best” depends on the biological question.

This atlas makes a strong but bounded case: Nanopore is the most information-rich choice when perturbations may alter isoforms, transcript structure, editing outcomes, RNA modifications or poly(A) tails. For very large, gene-level screens, DRUG-seq, Perturb-seq and imaging can offer higher throughput or lower cost. The most powerful programmes combine them.

01Nanopore perturbomics

When the response lives in the isoform, long reads change the experiment.

Short reads efficiently quantify genes. Nanopore preserves the molecular context needed to connect a perturbation to transcript structure and, in direct RNA experiments, native-RNA features.

Single-cell compatible

Sequence barcoded 10x cDNA with long reads, while short reads provide efficient cell and guide assignment.

Hidden consequences

Detect splice switching, intron retention and alternative polyadenylation even when gene-level expression barely changes.

06

Hybrid by design

Pair Nanopore depth where structure matters with Illumina scale, imaging or proteomics for broader phenotype coverage.

Single-cell CRISPR isoform sequencing

Stanford researchers combined short-read guide/gene-expression profiling with Nanopore cDNA sequencing to observe CRISPR-induced mutations and full-length isoforms at single-cell resolution.

  • Directly detects targeted edits
  • Resolves exon-skipping consequences
  • Public raw data and MIT-licensed code

CRISPore-seq

A multimodal CRISPR perturbation framework designed to capture guide identity, gene expression, full-length transcript isoforms and surface proteins.

  • Designed for scalable pooled screens

m6A-isoSC-seq and RNA surveillance

Long- and short-read single-cell sequencing were integrated with m6A mapping, while SMG1 inhibition and UPF1 depletion supplied mechanistic perturbations of RNA surveillance.

  • Isoform-level m6A context
  • SMG1 inhibitor direct-RNA arm
  • UPF1 knockdown dataset
